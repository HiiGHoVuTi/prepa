\begin{align}
f(e_{G}) &= e_{H}  \\
f^{-1}(\{ e_{H} \}) &= \{ e_{G} \}
\end{align}
$$

# $\impliedby$

Soient $x,x'$ tels que $f(x)=f(x')$. On a $f(x)^{-1} = f(x^{-1})$ d'o√π
$$
\begin{align}
f(x) * f(x^{-1}) &= f(x') *f(x^{-1}) \\
f(e) &= f(x' \cdot x^{-1})
\end{align}
$$
donc $x' \cdot x^{-1} \in \text{Ker} f$ donc $x' \cdot x^{-1} = e_{G}$ donc $x = x'$
